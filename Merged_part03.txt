A single mathematical object (the bridge operator) can encode both Einstein’s curvature and quantum field spectral data in a way that preserves their respective invariances exactly.

Resolution of a Century-Old Problem
The quantum gravity problem has historically been regarded as intractable due to:
Perturbative non-renormalizability of gravity as a quantum field.
Conceptual incompatibility between background-independent GR and background-dependent QFT.
Lack of experimentally accessible predictions in Planck-scale physics. The framework presented here overcomes all three:
Non-perturbative finiteness is achieved via absolute convergence of the spectral invariants.
Background independence is preserved through direct quantization of the metric via Spec(Dg) rather than perturbation around a fixed geometry.
Near-term falsifiable predictions are provided in gravitational wave astronomy, precision in-terferometry, and high-energy scattering.

Cosmology: The spectral invariants can be computed for Friedmann–Lemaˆıtre–Robertson–Walker spacetimes, yielding quantum corrections to early-universe expansion.
Gauge–Gravity Dualities: BQG may serve as an explicit constructive model for AdS/CFT-type correspondences, free of asymptotic boundary restrictions.
Condensed Matter Analogs: The Hilbert bundle formalism can be applied to emergent spacetime geometries in topological phases of matter, enabling laboratory analog tests.

This work represents:
The first explicit, parameter-free, background-independent, and testable theory of quantum gravity.
A demonstration that bridging invariants can circumvent longstanding theoretical barriers without loss of physical predictability.
A unification principle that could serve as a foundation for the next era of fundamental physics

By resolving quantum gravity in a fully explicit, mathematically rigorous, and empirically falsifiable manner, this framework:
Provides the definitive closure to one of the most challenging open problems in physics.
Introduces a new invariant-based methodology applicable across the entire landscape of the-oretical physics.
Offers a self-contained set of tools that can be independently verified, reproduced, and ex-tended by the scientific community.
The combination of theoretical elegance, mathematical completeness, and predictive experimental accessibility positions this result as a generational breakthrough.

Conclusion
We have presented a complete, parameter-free, and mathematically rigorous theory of quantum gravity that unifies General Relativity and Quantum Field Theory through an explicitly defined geometric–spectral bridge operator BQG.
This framework satisfies all of the following criteria simultaneously:
Exact recovery of GR and QFT in their respective low-energy limits.
Non-perturbative finiteness without background dependence.
Explicitly defined invariants ensuring diffeomorphism and unitarity preservation.
Near-term falsifiable predictions in gravitational wave dispersion, matter-wave interferometry, and high-energy scattering.
Freedom from arbitrary parameters — all constants are fixed by matching to known low-energy physics.
The explicit construction ensures that every component of the theory is independently checkable within the current mathematical framework, while its predictive content opens a clear experimental path to validation or falsification.
By overcoming the key theoretical and empirical barriers that have hindered progress for over a century, this work establishes a definitive resolution to the quantum gravity problem and introduces a unifying principle that is likely to reshape multiple domains of physics in the decades to come.

ABSTRACT

Motivated by results implying that the constituents of dark matter (DM) might be collisional, we consider a cosmological (toy-) model, in which the DM itself possesses some sort of thermodynamic properties. In this case, not only can the matter content of the Universe (the baryonic component, which is tightly gravitationally-bounded to the dark one, also being included) be treated as a classical gravitating fluid of positive pressure, but, together with all its other physical characteristics, the energy of this fluid’s internal motions should be taken into account as a source of the universal gravitational field. In principle, this form of energy can compensate for the extra (dark) energy, needed to compromise spatial flatness, while the post-recombination Universe remains ever-decelerating. What is more interesting, is that, at the same time (i.e., in the context of the collisional-DM approach), the theoretical curve representing the distance modulus as a function of the cosmological redshift, µ(z), fits the Hubble diagram of a multi-used sample of supernova Ia events quite accurately. A cosmological model filled with collisional DM could accommodate the majority of the currently-available observational data (including, also, those from baryon acoustic oscillations), without the need for either any dark energy (DE) or the cosmological constant. However, as we demonstrate, this is not the case for someone who, although living in a Universe filled with self-interacting DM, insists on adopting the traditional, collisionless-DM approach. From the point of view of this observer, the cosmologically-distant light-emitting sources seem to lie farther (i.e., they appear to be dimmer) than expected, while the Universe appears to be either accelerating or decelerating, depending on the value of the cosmological redshift. This picture, which, nowadays, represents the common perception in observational cosmology, acquires a more conventional interpretation within the context of the collisional-DM approach.

The luminosity distance and the distance modulus
Nowadays, the most direct and reliable method for determining, observationally, the (relatively) recent history of the Universe expansion, is to measure the redshift and the apparent luminos-ity (equivalently, the apparent magnitude, m) of cosmologically-distant indicators (standard candles), whose absolute luminos-ity (equivalently, the absolute magnitude, M) is assumed to be known.
SN Ia events constitute one of the most suitable cosmo-logical standard candles (Plionis et al. 2009). With the aid of these events, a number of scientific groups have attempted to find evidence in support of a recently-accelerating stage of the Universe (Garnavich et al. 1998; Riess et al. 1998, 2001, 2004, 2007; Perlmutter et al. 1999; Knop et al. 2003; Tonry et al. 2003; Astier et al. 2006; Wood-Vasey et al. 2007; Kowalski et al. 2008; Hicken et al. 2009). In each and every one of these sur-veys, the SN Ia events, at peak luminosity, appear to be dimmer (i.e., they seem to lie farther away) than expected. This result was, eventually, accommodated within the context of the concor-dance model, by a DE fluid of negative pressure, with ΩX 0.7 (see, e.g., Spergel et al. 2003; Tonry et al. 2003). However, in view of Eq. (47), there may be also another, more conventional interpretation.

Discussion
We have examined the possibility that the extra (dark) energy needed to flatten the Universe is represented by the energy of the internal motions of a collisional-DM fluid. Accordingly, we have considered the evolution of a cosmological (toy-) model driven by a gravitating fluid (consisting of DM – dominant – and bary-onic matter – subdominant) with thermodynamical content. As a consequence, the energy of this fluid’s internal motions has also been taken into account as a source of the universal gravitational field. Accordingly, we have asked ourselves, whether this model can also accommodate the apparent dimming of the cosmolog-ically distant indicators and the associated phase of accelerated expansion.
DM model as pressureless is questioned (Appendix B).
The debate between collisional- and collisionless-DM ap-proach is, definitely, in favor of the former. In particular, for every value of the cosmological redshift (z), as it is defined in the collisional-DM model, the corresponding collisionless-DM quantity, z˜, is always a little bit smaller (cf. Eq. (47)). As a con-sequence, in the collisional-DM model there is a characteristic value of the cosmological redshift, zc (cf. Eq. (52)), above which, the luminosity distance of the various light-emitting sources is always higher than what is inferred by an observer who treats the DM as pressureless (cf. Eq. (51)). In other words, from the point of view of someone who (although living in a collisional-DM model) insists on adopting the (traditional) collisionless-DM ap-proach, the cosmologically-distant indicators, located at z > zc, seem to lie farther away (i.e., appear dimmer) than expected (cf., also, Eq. (55)).
The similarity between the characteristic value zc and the (observationally-traced) transition redshift, zt, which, according to a supporter of the collisionless-DM scenario, signals the onset of the dimming of the SNe Ia standard candles, is obvious.
On the other hand, after the thermodynamical content of a collisional-DM fluid is taken into account, the theoretical curve representing the distance modulus, µ(z) (now given by Eq. (55)), fits the Hubble diagram of an extended sample of SN Ia standard candles quite accurately (green solid line in Fig. 1), in contrast to the corresponding collisionless-DM quantity, µ˜(z˜), given ei-ther (appropriately) by the combination of Eqs. (54) and (72) (orange solid line in Fig. 1) or (incorrectly) by the combination of Eqs. (54) and (73) (dashed line in Fig. 1). In fact, a cosmo-logical model filled with collisional DM could accommodate the majority of the currently-available observational data, including, also, those related to BAO, without the need for any dark energy or the cosmological constant (cf. Eqs. (81) and/or (82)).

At the same time, from the point of view of an observer who treats the DM as collisionless, the Universe appears to be either accelerating or decelerating, depending on the value of the cos-mological redshift (cf. Eq. (67)).
In this case, the quantity w, which, in the collisional-DM approach, parameterizes the various flows, also plays another (more interesting) role. As we have found, for wΩM 0.0714, there exists a (theoretically-determined) transition value, z˜t, of the (collisionless-DM-oriented) cosmological redshift, z˜, such that, for z˜ < z˜t, we have q˜ < 0, i.e., from the point of view of someone who adopts the (traditional) collisionless-DM ap-proach, the Universe is accelerating, without the need for any DE or the cosmological constant. This result is in complete agreement with what is implied by Fig. 2, i.e., w 1 . In other words, compatibility of the collisional-DM treatment under study with the observational data currently available, suggests that the DM itself consists of relativistic particles (hot DM).
In conclusion, the assumption that the DM constituents can be both collisional and relativistic, could provide a reasonable and conventional explanation for several open aspects of modern cosmology, including: (i) the extra (dark) energy needed to flat-ten the Universe, which can be compensated by the energy of the internal motions of the collisional-DM fluid; (ii) the observed dimming of the SNe Ia standard candles and the apparent accel-erated expansion of the Universe, both of which might be due to the misinterpretation of several cosmologically relevant parame-ters by those observers who, although living in a collisional-DM Universe, insist on adopting the collisionless-DM approach. The absence of any ad hoc fine-tuning in our study, makes the above (purely theoretical) results very promising.
In spite of all the above advantages of our model, we have to point out that this is, definitely, a toy model. The collisional-DM approach, developed in this article, can (and should) be debated, at least, along the following lines:
The collisional-DM treatment of the Universe does not al-leviate the age problem, but, rather, makes it harder. In the model given by Eq. (28), the coordinate time, t, is related to the corresponding conformal quantity (η) by

For the time being, the con-ventional theory of hot DM does not appear to conform with the large-scale structure of the Universe (see, e.g., Hooper 2009), although there are recent studies that ap-pear to challenge this result (see, e.g., Farrar & Peebles 2004; Gubser & Peebles 2004). Nevertheless, a hot-DM model looks much less exotic than most of the (currently-investigated) DE scenarios.
In any case, the assumption that the Universe matter content (ba-sically its DM component) can be collisional (in the sense that it also possesses some sort of thermodynamical content), is to be seen as a natural effort to take into account all the (so far, prac-tically, neglected) internal physical characteristics of a classical cosmological fluid as sources of the universal gravitational field. As we have shown, under this assumption, one can compen-sate for the majority of the recent observational data, inferring that Ω	1, as well as the “unexpected” dimming of the SNe Ia standard candles and the “apparent” accelerated expansion of the Universe, not to mention the compatibility with BAO’s data anal-ysis, without the need for any DE or the cosmological constant, and (certainly) without suffering from the coincidence problem. From the point of view of someone who (although living in the collisional-DM model) insists on adopting the collisionless-DM
approach, an inflection point (in the d˜L versus z diagram) arises,
anyway, at relatively low values of the cosmological redshift.
Although speculative, the idea that the DE (needed to flat-ten the Universe) could be attributed to the internal motions of a collisional-DM fluid, is (at least) intriguing and should be ex-plored further and scrutinized in the search for conventional al-ternatives to the DE concept.

Hence, DM must decline at higher z-parameters of red-shift, while the presence of black holes must depress the presence of DM (core-cusp problem).
The gravitational field produced by this hypothesized DM results in agreement with the MOND model.
E/perimental observations of merging gala/y clusters justify this representation.

A Discrete Universal Medium: An Alternative Ontological Framework for Matter, Energy, Fields, and Gravitation
Author: Hikmat Alakbarov Hasan
Affiliation: Independent Researcher
Keywords: ontology of physics, discrete medium, field theory, quantum foundations, determinism, process ontology, gravitation, electromagnetic propagation

Abstract
This paper proposes an alternative ontological model for the fundamental structure of physical reality. Contrary to the standard view in modern physics, which treats “vacuum” as an empty background or as the lowest-energy state of quantum fields, the present model asserts that true emptiness does not exist. Instead, all space is filled with a discrete universal medium composed of elementary process units that interact through field-like synchronizing mechanisms. Within this framework, matter, energy, fields, particles, light, and gravitation emerge as different manifestations of processes occurring within this medium. The model is deterministic,
process-based, and eliminates the conceptual dualism between particles and fields. The paper formulates the axioms of the model, derives its conceptual consequences, and outlines its potential to resolve several paradoxes in quantum mechanics and field theory.

Introduction
Modern physics describes nature through two major frameworks: quantum field theory (QFT) and general relativity (GR). QFT treats fields as fundamental and particles as quantized excitations of these fields, while GR interprets gravitation as curvature of space time. Despite their empirical success, these frameworks face conceptual challenges:

the ontological status of the vacuum,
the duality between particles and waves,
the measurement problem in quantum mechanics,
the nonlocal correlations observed in entanglement,
the incompatibility between GR and QFT at fundamental scales.

This paper introduces a unified ontological model that reinterprets these issues through a single assumption: there is no empty space. Instead, the universe is permeated by a discrete, dynamic, elastic medium whose internal processes give rise to all observable physical phenomena.

This approach is not a revival of classical aether theories. The proposed medium is not static, continuous, or mechanically rigid. It is a process-based, dynamic substrate whose behavior is governed by local interactions and deterministic evolution.

/
Axiomatic Foundations of the Model
The model is built on six fundamental axioms.

Axiom 1. Existence of a Universal Medium
What is commonly called “vacuum” is not empty. It consists of discrete elementary processes that fill all space.

Axiom 2. Discreteness
The medium is composed of minimal process units. These units are not particles in the classical sense but localized, quantized processes.

Axiom 3. Field-Like Interaction
Elementary processes interact through synchronizing mechanisms analogous to fields. A field is not a separate entity but a process that coordinates the behavior of the medium.

Axiom 4. Determinism
There is no fundamental randomness. At every moment, each process evolves according to the strongest local influence. Apparent probabilistic behavior arises from incomplete knowledge of the medium’s full state.

Axiom 5. Matter and Energy
Matter is a stable, harmonious configuration of processes within the medium. Energy is the intensity of these processes.

Axiom 6. Gravitation
Gravitation results from variations in the density and tension of the medium. It is not a force or curvature of spacetime but a structural property of the medium.

Redefining Fundamental Physical Concepts
Matter
Matter is not a substance separate from space. It is a stable, self-sustaining pattern within the universal medium. Its properties arise from the way local processes resonate with the surrounding medium.

Energy

Energy is the degree of activity or intensity of a process. It is not an independent entity but a measure of how strongly a process influences the medium.

Fields
A field is a synchronization process that coordinates the behavior of elementary units in the medium. Fields are not ontologically distinct from matter; they are modes of interaction within the same substrate.

Elementary Particles
Particles are the smallest stable process units of the medium. They are not point-like objects but localized patterns of activity.

Light
Light is the propagation of a process through the medium, not the movement of a particle. A photon is not an object traveling through space but a sequence of changes transmitted across the medium.

Gravitation
Gravitation emerges from gradients in the medium’s density and tension. Objects follow paths determined by these gradients, producing effects analogous to gravitational attraction.

Physical Consequences of the Model
Absence of Photon Trajectories
Since light is a process, not a particle, it does not follow a classical trajectory. This naturally explains the wave-particle duality and the behavior observed in double-slit experiments.

Medium-Dependent Speed of Light
The speed of light depends on the density and elasticity of the medium. This aligns with observed variations in refractive index and propagation speed in different materials.

Deterministic Explanation of Quantum Probabilities
Quantum probabilities arise from incomplete knowledge of the medium’s full state. The underlying dynamics are deterministic.

Interference and Observer Effects
Measurement alters the local configuration of the medium, which in turn modifies interference patterns. This provides a physical explanation for the observer effect without invoking wavefunction collapse.

Unified View of Forces
All forces are manifestations of the medium’s internal dynamics. Electromagnetism, gravitation, and possibly other interactions emerge from different modes of process synchronization.

Comparison with Existing Theories
Quantum Field Theory
QFT treats fields as fundamental; this model treats fields as emergent processes.
QFT allows vacuum fluctuations; this model denies the existence of vacuum as emptiness.
QFT is probabilistic; this model is deterministic.

General Relativity
GR interprets gravitation as spacetime curvature; this model interprets it as medium tension.
GR has no underlying substrate; this model posits a discrete substrate.

Classical Aether Theories
The proposed medium is not a classical aether:

it is discrete, not continuous,
dynamic, not static,
process-based, not material,
compatible with relativistic invariance (to be developed in future work).

Future Directions
To develop this model into a full physical theory, several steps are required:

Mathematical formulation of the medium’s discrete dynamics.
Elasticity equations describing density and tension variations.
Propagation equations for process transmission (light).
Deterministic analogues of quantum behavior.
Testable predictions that distinguish this model from QFT and GR.

Conclusion
This paper presents a unified ontological framework in which matter, energy, fields, particles, light, and gravitation are all manifestations of processes occurring within a discrete universal medium. By eliminating the concept of empty space and grounding all physical phenomena in

the dynamics of this medium, the model offers a coherent and deterministic alternative to the standard interpretations of modern physics. It provides conceptual clarity, resolves several foundational paradoxes, and opens new directions for theoretical and experimental exploration.
