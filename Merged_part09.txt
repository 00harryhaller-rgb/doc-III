CONCLUSION AND DISCUSSION

In this paper, we analyzed the implications of a dark QCD sector with a confinement scale ОӣD	10вҲ’3 10вҲ’4 eV. Continuing the analysis of the model recently proposed in [15], we have demonstrated that DE can arise from the dynamical formation of dark strong con-densates, due to the breaking of a dark chiral symme-try. On the other hand, the low confinement scale al-lows the formation of exotic dark stars with a mass of
7

10вҲ’14 10вҲ’15MвҠҷ. These objects are so small to be eluded by present limits from gravitational microlensing and cluster limits. We are then tempted to dub these new objects Mini-MACHOs.
We also demonstrated how the current abundance of DM can be easily recovered without the need of WIMP miracles or non-thermal mechanisms. This new paradigm for a unifying picture of DE and DM suggests several ob-servables with respect to the standard picture. From the DE side, the dynamical evolution of the condensate en-ergy density could be detected in future experiments test-ing late Universe expansion. On the other hand, Mini-MACHOs could be detected in more accurate microlens-ing experiments, or searching for by-fly anomalies in our solar system. It is interesting that both DE and DM are governed by the same scale of Dark Chiral Symmetry Breaking.
Let us comment that our scenario is capable of predicting gravitational waves signals detectable by LIGO/VIRGO collaboration in the near future. In fact, even if the gravitational masses of dark exotic stars are so small, they are spread out in the dark halo with a much higher number and density then ordinary stars. In partic-ular, such as rapid supernovae events in the dark sector can release characteristic signals with very different fea-tures compared to ordinary supernovae. On the other hand, the presence of a large number of Mini-MACHOs in the dark halo can increase the probability of dark bi-naries emitting gravitational waves.
Of course, a further theoretical question worth address-ing is: Why does the dark QCD sector contribute to the vacuum energy while standard QCD is somehow to be screened by an unknown mechanism? A possible expla-nation could be related to an unknown UV completion of our model. Inspired by Ref. [37], we are tempted to suggest that if the SM is localized on a Brane-world or a domain wall in higher dimensions while IQCD is local-ized on a Dark parallel D-Brane-World or Dark domain wall, the vacuum energy can be differently subtracted by different D-Brane/Wall tensions or different RR or NS-NS fluxes in the Calabi-Yau internal compactifications wrapped by branes.

Remark 6. One of the reviewers raised two questions. First, he wondered about the logic to combine a low energy event, the quantization of the fields of the standard model with a flat metric, with an high-energy event, the quantization of gravity. As we have already pointed out in the introduction, a unified quantization of gravity and matter fields leads to a hyperbolic equation of second order in a fiber space, where the main part of the hyperbolic operator acts in the fibers. The zero-order terms of the operator contain the contributions of the quantized matter Hamiltonian and the interaction of gravity with matter fields occurs with the help of the fiber variables (t, Пғij). The metric Пғij is used in the quantization of the matter fields. Looking at the spatial eigenfunction v of the gravitational Hamiltonian and its eigenvalue, which expresses the energy, then the eigenvalue is independent of the metric Пғij at which v is evaluated and only the evaluation point is relevant for the interaction, i.e., even if a non-flat metric Пғij would have been used in the quantization of the matter fields, the contribution to the unified operator would not have changed qualitatively. Furthermore, as we already mentioned in the introduction, due to the scalar curvature term R, we cannot expect to solve the WheelerвҖ“DeWitt equation for all (t, Пғij) if we use the separation of variables, instead, we have to choose metrics with constant scalar curvatures. Thus, we opted for Пғij = Оҙij, also out of necessity because we could not quantize the matter field in the curved spacetime.
The second interaction with respect to the variable t, the quantum time, is realized in the ODE, where the contributions by the spatial gravitational resp. matter eigenfunctions and also by the cosmological constant Оӣ have a power of t as a multiplicative factor with different exponents. For small t, the gravitational energy dominates because of the factor tвҖ”2, for larger t, the matter energy dominates because of the factor tвҖ” 2 , and if Оӣ = 0, then the cosmological constant dominates for very large t because of the factor t2. This is also reflected in the results of Lemmas 4 and 5.
The second question raised concerned the QFT renormalizability in this unified setting.
The quantization of gravity takes place in the fibers of E while the quantization of the matter fields takes place in the base space ВЈ0 = Rn, which we equipped with the Euclidean metric for this task. Hence, the usual renormalization techniques can be used to deal with infinities. The fibers are ignored in this process.

Discussion
Two places to discuss the speed of light
Two points of view on the speed of light, corresponding to two scales of observation, are adopted in the foregoing.
First of all, there's the local world of terrestrial observers. The word local extends to the space around us as far as the sun (150 million km). On this scale, we relate to a speed of light "fixed" by convention at the value c012. This is the value used in special relativity and its Euclidean space, where we describe increments of space and time by the small amplitudes dx, dy, dz and dt. It's normal to relate everything to our space and time standards based on the reference c0, including distant objects. Angular distances, or luminosity distances in cosmology, correspond to a "projection" into a supposedly Euclidean universe such as our own, but they do not refer to the actual path taken by the light that reaches us. We need to define translation coefficients, and this is where the second point of view comes in.
The cosmological point of view corresponds to a scale where the representative elementary volume has a side equal to the light year, larger by a factor of 105 than the previous local scale. It's as if we could look at the universe from the outside, with the appearance of a homogeneous fluid. At this scale, if we relate it to the c0 of our base, light travels at a speed c0 /Оұ. This happens as in a refractive medium, where we also consider a "macroscopic" velocity different from the local velocity13. Various images can support this lower speed, already hidden in electromagnetism in the macroscopic index; here it would be, for example, that of a gravitational zig-zag.


This duality of points of view is comparable to that of a walker who estimates distances to other people by their angular size. If the space separating them from him is open and empty, he gives a first estimate of the speed to reach them, which will be the rhythm of his step. But if there's a crowd, even if the speed increment is the same from one person to the next (still the rhythm of his step), the "total" speed will be less, because of all the little detours to be made in the progression. It would be a mistake for the walker to estimate the overall speed as equal to the invariable speed adopted locally. He keeps the latter as a gauge, but has to assess its relationship

with the speed experienced along the entire route. Similarly, in a refractive medium like water, the speed of a photon is c0 from atom to atom on the nanometric scale, but it would be a mistake to assign this value to the speed of light arriving from metric distances14.


Mathematical formalism allows us to distinguish between - 1) the local scale, in the tangent plane to the curved space (that of the ensemble scale, see below); here we define the increments of distances and durations dx, dy, dz, dt measured by the standard motion c0 . - 2) space in its cosmological dimension, where lengths are measured by weighting dx, dy, dz, dt by the gij coefficients of a metric. 9.2 Back to gravitational mirages and Einstein's equations
The distinction between scales (local scale, and its velocity c0; cosmological scale, velocity cc) allows us to clarify a question we could, or should, have asked ourselves. It concerns the replacement of c = c0 by cc, both in the gravitational mirage equations (section 5) and in Einstein's (section 6). Why do it? Isn't refractivity (bringing velocity cc) already accommodated by the coefficients gij, as seen in the previous section with the Schwartzchild metric? In response, however, it seems to us that we have done the right thing.


For gravitational deflection first of all, the curved path from objects commonly observed by astronomers is understandable on a cosmological scale. But the calculation is made in general relativity as if the deflecting mass were alone, bypassed by a photon at speed c0, and not bathed in the matter-populated universe. But in this case, as we have shown, we need to consider a global velocity equal to cc. Without repeating all the calculations, we can see that a good approximation of what is happening is provided by replacing c = c0 by cc in equation (4).

The work is intended as an invitation for critique and refinement rather than a definitive alternative to standard cosmology.

Keywords: scalar fields; early universe; threshold dynamics; vacuum structure; General Rela-tivity; speculative cosmology.

Introduction
The standard cosmological model, often summarised as ОӣCDM, has enjoyed remarkable success in explaining a wide range of observations, from the cosmic microwave background (CMB) to large-scale structure and the expansion history of the Universe. Within this framework, General Relativity (GR) provides the gravitational sector, while dark matter, dark energy, and baryonic physics account for structure formation and late-time acceleration. Nonetheless, a number of conceptual and observational tensions remain, especially in the context of the very early Universe and high-redshift structure.
